about me.


I've always loved building things, since I was a kid. Put effort, creativity, and analytical thinking into building things I would be proud of. Time passed, life happened, and I walked down a path that made sense. I had 2 international study and work experiences and graduated in nutrition and dietetics. Later on I created a product and a business and joined another, growing both of them 300%. In addition to providing me with wonderful memories, all of these experiences have enhanced my skill sets, especially communication, adaptability, and collaborative skills.

I've always been curious about software development, but I never considered it as a path to take. At one point in my life, I couldn't imagine my future. I had no goals, the days seemed the same, I had no challenges. Until one day, I started thinking, why not? I thought it was now or never, now or spend the rest of my life thinking about how it would have been. A few weeks later, at age 31, I sold my company and began studying the most efficient way I could. Started by learning how to learn and diving into programming fundamentals. A turning point for me was when I joined Harvard's CS50 course.

Fast-forward, and it seemed that the happiness and satisfaction in building things with Lego blocks that I had as a kid was slowly coming back. This time as colored characters in a dark-themed code editor. Now I had the answer to many of the holes in my life, and I finally could foresee my future and goals. I get excited about the possibility of working with something that keeps me motivated, curious and requires constant pursuit of knowledge and the use of both logical and creative reasoning. Additionally, the ones and zeros and the World Wide Web are the greatest collective inventions ever made by mankind. There is no way to feel more alive.

In my free time, I love staying active and healthy through road cycling, running, and eating well. I find joy in the little things and always make time for the things that matter to me. I enjoy quiet moments of solitude and mindfulness, as well as the satisfaction of slow-paced tasks such as carpentry and bike maintenance. However, I also relish the joy of cooking and sharing a glass of wine with loved ones. On top of that, I love to code and create something just for fun. No pressure, just a chance to challenge myself and be creative.



With a love for building and striving to bring creative solutions to the table,
I am ready to tackle new challenges and open to opportunities in remote, on-site, and freelance projects.
